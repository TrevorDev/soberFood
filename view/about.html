What is SoberFood?

For young-adult shoppers lacking know-how concerning effective shopping and home-inventory methods to reduce food waste, our SoberFood grocery traceability game provides a fun and interactive platform to make informed choices about how to shop, meal plan, cook, and consume food.

Food waste that occurs on a local level has negative impact on the socioeconomic environment in which we live. In a city of over 122,000 people, if 1 in 10 people are food insecure in Canada (Food Banks Canada, 2013), this amounts to nearly 12,000 persons in our Guelph community. At the same time, it is estimated the average Canadian throws away about $28 worth of the groceries they purchase each week (Gillis, 2013). Clearly, there exists a disparity in food availability and utilization between those engaging in food purchase, & those who are less fortunate & hence living as food insecure.

As a solution to the challenge detailed for the 2014 Food Waste Hack-A-Thon of combatting food waste on the University of Guelph campus, our student team proposes the creation of game-driven, web-based platform entitled ‘SoberFood’ to educate the student body in an interactive and web-based fashion. Our proposal can easily be extended horizontally across the food value chain, as the core elements of inventory tracing, consumption data and waste monitoring are also applicable to larger institutions. However, by beginning at the level of the consumer, we are addressing the more immediate issue on our campus and Canadian society at large, considering 51% of food waste in Canada occurs in the home (Statistics Canada, 2010).
